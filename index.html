👋 About Me

I am Mridusmita Nath, a PhD researcher working in the field of Computational Chemistry. My research focuses on excited-state photophysics, singlet fission, molecular spintronics, and data-driven discovery of advanced organic materials.

My work integrates quantum chemistry, machine learning, and first-principles simulations to understand electronic and transport properties of molecular systems.

🔬 Research Interests

Excited State Photophysics

Understanding low-lying excited states in organic conjugated molecules for optoelectronic applications.

Singlet Fission Materials

Design and computational screening of molecules capable of efficient singlet fission for solar energy applications.

Spintronics

Spin-dependent transport properties of molecular junctions using NEGF-DFT methods.

Machine Learning for Materials

Using molecular descriptors and machine learning models to predict excited-state properties of organic molecules.
