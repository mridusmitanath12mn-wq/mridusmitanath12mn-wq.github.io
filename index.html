👋 About Me

I am Mridusmita Nath, a PhD researcher working in the field of Computational Chemistry. My research focuses on excited-state photophysics, singlet fission, molecular spintronics, and data-driven discovery of advanced organic materials.

My work integrates quantum chemistry, machine learning, and first-principles simulations to understand electronic and transport properties of molecular systems.

🔬 Research Interests

Excited State Photophysics

Low-lying excited states of organic conjugated molecules for optoelectronic applications.

Singlet Fission

Design and computational screening of materials capable of efficient singlet fission.

Molecular Spintronics

Spin-dependent transport properties of molecular junctions using NEGF-DFT.

Machine Learning

ML models and molecular descriptors for predicting excited-state properties.
